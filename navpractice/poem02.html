Two

Almost a girl it was and issued forth

from this concordant joy of song and lyre,

and clearly shining through her springtime veils

she made herself a bed inside my ear.

And slept in me. And all things ere her sleep.

rThe trees I always marveled at, those

feelable distances, the meadow felt

and every wondering that befell myself,

She slept the world. You singing god, how

did you so perfect her that she did not crave

first to be awake? See, she arose and slept.

Where is her death? O will you yet invent

this theme before your song consumes itself?

Whither is she sinking out of me? ... A girl almost...
